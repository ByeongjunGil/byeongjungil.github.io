01

About

I am a PhD candidate in Materials Science and Engineering at Seoul National University, working with Prof. Miyoung Kim. My research resolves atomic- and nanoscale phenomena in functional materials by developing and applying advanced scanning transmission electron microscopy.

The materials I care about are the ones conventional microscopy struggles with: they degrade within seconds of exposure, or hide their order parameters below the contrast of standard imaging. I use low-dose acquisition, 4D-STEM, and electron ptychography to capture pristine structure and the pathways by which it breaks down.

Low-dose (S)TEM

Imaging beam-sensitive halide perovskites and complex oxides before the electron beam rewrites them.

In situ cryo-(S)TEM

Watching temperature-driven structural and order-parameter transitions in multiferroic and quantum materials, live.

Electron ptychography

Resolving point defects and light elements that conventional STEM modes simply cannot reach.
